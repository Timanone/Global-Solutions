FAQ

Want to Know More?

Key commercial, logistics, and documentation topics

What is your minimum order volume (MOQ)?

MOQ depends on product category, availability, and destination. Most wholesale requests start from pallet-level volumes. Share your destination and estimated volume to receive a precise quote.

Which product categories do you supply?

We support multi-category supply across spirits, wine, beer, and non-alcoholic beverages, subject to availability and market requirements.

Do you support mixed pallets and multi-category orders?

In many cases, yes. Mixed formats depend on warehouse handling rules, product type, and route. We confirm feasibility during quotation.

What is the typical timeline from inquiry to dispatch?

Timeline depends on availability, documentation readiness, and destination. After terms are confirmed and payment is received, dispatch is coordinated via the selected warehouse workflow.

How do you issue quotations and proforma invoices?

We provide structured offers and proforma invoices defining product scope, volumes, destination, trade terms, and validity period. Quotations reflect availability at the time of request.

Do you reserve stock after a quote is issued?

Stock reservation depends on the supplier or warehouse workflow and payment terms. Where reservation is possible, we confirm the timeline and conditions in the offer.

Do you provide batch / lot traceability information?

Yes. Where applicable, batch or lot information can be provided in line with warehouse documentation and shipment records, subject to product type and transaction structure.

What payment terms do you support?

Payment terms depend on the agreed deal structure and counterparty profile. Standard practice is aligned with confirmed documentation flow and commercial conditions.

Which Incoterms do you support?

We commonly work with EXW, FCA, CPT, and CIP depending on destination and logistics setup. If needed, we recommend the most practical option for your route.

Do you coordinate transport or work with the buyer's forwarder?

Both options are possible. We can coordinate logistics through supply chain partners or work with your nominated forwarder, subject to warehouse release and documentation requirements.

From where do you dispatch (warehousing / locations)?

Dispatch is coordinated through regulated warehousing infrastructure and partners. The specific workflow is defined per transaction and destination requirements.

What documents can you provide for export shipments?

Documentation depends on the trade model and route, but typically includes commercial invoicing and shipment documentation required for cross-border execution. The final set is confirmed per deal.

What information do you require from buyers (KYC)?

For a structured workflow, we collect standard B2B counterparty details, including company information, responsible contact, destination market, and trade context.

Can you work with escrow / controlled release workflows?

Yes, where applicable. Transaction structure and release steps depend on warehouse and counterparty requirements and are confirmed during quotation.

Do you work only with licensed importers / distributors?

We work with professional B2B buyers operating under applicable regulatory requirements in their destination markets. Where licensing is mandatory, buyers must ensure local compliance.

Which regions do you primarily serve?

We support export-oriented supply to selected international markets, including the European Union, the Middle East, Asia, and other regions, subject to route feasibility.

Are there destinations you do not serve?

Yes. Certain destinations may be restricted due to regulatory, banking, or route limitations. Feasibility is confirmed after reviewing your request.

Can you share price lists publicly?

We do not publish public retail-style price lists. Pricing is provided upon inquiry and depends on volumes, destination, category, and availability.

What bank fee type should be used (SHA / OUR)?

Fee type depends on the banking route and agreed terms. Clarifying fee allocation upfront helps avoid delays and underpayment issues.

How quickly do you respond to inquiries?

We respond within 1 business day during working hours (Mon–Fri). Requests with complete details are processed faster.

What should I include to receive a fast quote?

Destination market, product categories or specific items (if known), estimated volume, preferred Incoterms, and your company details. This allows us to prepare a precise and timely quotation.

How do you handle claims (shortages / damages)?

Claims are handled according to confirmed warehouse release conditions and transport responsibility under the agreed Incoterms. We recommend reporting issues immediately upon receipt with supporting photos.
